Martin Luther King Jr.

1929-1968

Martin Luther King Jr. was a babtist minister and the most renowned civil rights activist. On August 28, 1963, 6 organizations organized the March on Washington, with King representing the SCLC as president, at which he gave his famous "I have a dream" speech. A year and a half later he partnered with the SNCC at the Selma Marches to protest the protection of their right to vote. Then, on April 4, 1968, Martin Luther King Jr. was assasinated.

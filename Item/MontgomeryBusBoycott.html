Montgomery Bus Boycott

Not takin' the bus is bussin'

Dec. 1955-Dec. 1956

4 days after Rosa Parks was arrested for not giving up her seat, the Montgomery Bus Boycotts began. During which, colored people, who made up 75% of the bus passengers, refused to ride the bus. The protest ended after Browder v. Gayle took effect declaring segregated buses as unconstitutional.
